Mastering Low Light Photography

Shooting in the dark doesn't have to mean grainy, unusable photos. Whether you are capturing the neon glow of a city street at midnight or an intimate wedding reception, understanding how to manipulate your camera's sensor in low light is crucial.

The first step is dropping your aperture as low as it can go. A prime lens opening up to f/1.4 or f/1.8 is your best friend here. It allows maximum light to hit the sensor, meaning you can keep your ISO lower and avoid that dreaded digital noise.

Next, stabilize. If you are shooting below 1/60th of a second, any micro-movement from your hands will blur the shot. If a tripod isn't an option, tuck your elbows into your chest, exhale completely, and roll your finger over the shutter button rather than jabbing it.
