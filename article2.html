The Manhattan Wedding

Shooting a wedding in the heart of New York City is an entirely different beast than a quiet countryside ceremony. The energy is electric, the schedule is tight, and the lighting changes every time you turn a corner.

For this 12-hour shoot, portability was key. I stuck to a two-camera harness setup. One body held a versatile 24-70mm lens to capture the wide architecture of the venue and group shots, while the other was locked with a 50mm prime for intimate, candid portraits.

The biggest challenge was the transition from the bright, midday sun during the rooftop first-look to the dimly lit, moody reception hall. Trusting my gear and knowing exactly how far I could push my ISO without losing detail saved the day.
